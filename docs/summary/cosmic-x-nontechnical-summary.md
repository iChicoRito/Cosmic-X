# CosmicX: Non-Technical Summary

## What CosmicX Is

CosmicX is an interactive space-exploration website that lets people experience two connected stories:

1. **Become the Creator** — explore and experiment with a living model of planets, moons, galaxies, asteroids, comets, and black holes.
2. **Before the Stars** — travel through the history of the universe, from the Big Bang to the distant future.

It is designed as a visual learning and discovery experience. Users do not need to install software, create an account, or understand astronomy formulas before using it.

## Who It Is For

CosmicX can support:

- Students learning about planets, motion, gravity, impacts, and cosmic history.
- Teachers demonstrating space concepts in a more visual and interactive way.
- Space enthusiasts who want to explore and experiment.
- General audiences who prefer learning through animation and direct interaction.
- Developers or researchers who need a visual demonstration platform, while understanding that it is not a precision scientific instrument.

## What Users Can Do

### Explore the Solar System and Other Galaxies

Users can:

- Visit the Milky Way, an imagined planetary system in Andromeda, and a system around the black hole in Messier 87.
- Select planets, moons, stars, and other objects to inspect them.
- Read object information such as size, mass, gravity, temperature, atmosphere, orbital period, and moon count.
- View planetary orbits, labels, asteroid belts, stars, nebulas, dust, atmospheres, rings, and moon systems.
- Watch the simulation continue forward, pause it, reverse it, or jump to another date.
- explore dates up to about 100 years before or after the model's starting point.

### Change the Simulation

Users can act as the creator of the system by:

- Changing simulation speed, gravity strength, planet size, and displayed distances.
- Showing or hiding orbit trails and object labels.
- Turning collision behavior, eclipse events, and nearby asteroid activity on or off.
- Triggering a solar eclipse.
- Spawning blue or green comets with adjustable orbits.
- Spawning asteroids from the camera, into random orbits, or near Earth.
- Adjusting an asteroid's size, mass, and velocity.
- Inspecting spawned asteroids and starting a mining operation.
- Creating black holes with adjustable mass.
- Launching asteroids or comets toward selected targets.
- Adjusting projectile speed, approach angle, mass, size, and target guidance.
- Triggering meteor showers.
- Watching impacts create craters, debris, destruction, or orbital changes depending on their strength.
- Clearing created objects or resetting the entire simulation.

### Control the View

CosmicX includes six ways to view the Solar sandbox:

- **Orbit** — circle, pan, and zoom around the system.
- **Free Flight** — fly freely using the keyboard and mouse.
- **Follow** — travel with a selected object.
- **Cinematic** — watch an automatically directed tour.
- **Drone** — use a close, gently moving observation view.
- **Telescope** — observe a selected target with adjustable magnification.

Users can also focus objects by clicking them, reset the camera, hide the interface for an uninterrupted view, collapse information panels, and use fullscreen mode.

### Travel Through the History of the Universe

The Big Bang experience presents eleven major stages:

1. Singularity
2. Planck Epoch
3. Inflation
4. Particle Formation
5. Atoms Form
6. First Stars
7. First Galaxies
8. Milky Way Forms
9. Solar System Forms
10. Present Day
11. Future Universe

Users can:

- Play the complete cosmic journey.
- Pause, reverse, speed up, or slow down the experience.
- Drag through the cosmic timeline.
- Jump directly to any stage.
- Read the name, time, temperature, and explanation for each stage.
- Adjust how quickly the universe appears to expand.
- Switch between a guided cinematic camera and manual camera control.
- Hide the interface, replay the journey, or return to the mode menu.

## Main Benefits

### Easier Understanding

CosmicX turns difficult ideas such as planetary motion, gravity, cosmic expansion, impacts, and the age of the universe into visible experiences.

### Learning Through Experimentation

Users can change conditions and immediately observe the results. This encourages curiosity, comparison, and cause-and-effect learning.

### One Connected Space Experience

The Solar sandbox and Big Bang journey are part of one website. Moving between its screens feels continuous instead of opening separate pages.

### Flexible Presentation

The interface works for personal exploration, classroom demonstrations, fullscreen presentations, and mobile or desktop viewing.

### Adjustable Visual Performance

Users can select lower or higher visual quality, change effects, and control how much visual detail appears.

### Accessible Interaction

Controls include readable labels, keyboard-friendly elements, status announcements, reduced-motion support, and layouts that adapt to smaller screens.

### No Account or Installation Required

The application runs in a modern web browser and does not require sign-in, payment, or local installation.

## Important Expectations

CosmicX is an educational and exploratory simulation. It uses scaled distances, sizes, timing, visual effects, reference facts, and simplified behavior to make the experience understandable and interactive. It should not be used for spacecraft navigation, professional astronomical prediction, or other research requiring precision data.

Planet textures normally load from online sources. When those images are unavailable, CosmicX creates replacement textures so the experience can continue. User preferences can be remembered in the same browser, but created objects and simulation sessions are not saved as permanent accounts or cloud records.

## Plain-Language Term Guide

| Term | Meaning in CosmicX |
| --- | --- |
| Solar sandbox | The interactive area where users explore and change planetary systems |
| Cosmic timeline | The guided history of the universe |
| Simulation | A working visual model of how objects or events behave |
| Orbit | The path one object follows around another |
| Gravity | The attraction that pulls objects toward one another |
| Collision | Two simulated objects striking each other |
| Impact warning | A notice that two objects may collide soon |
| Epoch | A major period in the history of the universe |
| Expansion | The increasing distance between large regions of the universe |
| Cinematic camera | A view automatically guided by the application |
| Fullscreen | A display mode that fills the screen |
| Visual quality | The amount of graphical detail and effects shown |
